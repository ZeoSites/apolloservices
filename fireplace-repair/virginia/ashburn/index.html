Fireplace Maintenance Tips

Looking after your fireplace is essential for both well-being and efficiency. Periodic upkeep ensures your fireplace runs optimally, reducing the hazard of risks and prolonging its lifespan. Here are some valuable fireplace maintenance recommendations to help you keep your fireplace in top condition:

1. Regular Cleaning

Remove ashes regularly using a hearth tool. Retain a small layer of ash to shield the firebox. You can book a expert chimney sweep to remove soot and creosote buildup every year. This lessens the hazard of fires in the chimney.

2. Check for Damage

Regularly check the firebox for cracks, loose bricks, or signs of wear and repair any damage without delay to maintain architectural integrity. Review the external of the chimney for cracks, loose or missing bricks, and signs of water damage, and address any issues immediately.

3. Ensure Proper Ventilation

Ensure the damper is operating correctly. Open it before starting a fire and close it when the fireplace is not in use to stop heat loss. You can test the draft by starting a small piece of paper and holding it near the damper. The smoke should go up up the chimney.

4. Monitor and Maintain the Flue

Consider installing a flue cap to keep debris, animals, and rain out of the flue. Check the flue for obstructions, verifying it's unobstructed for proper ventilation.

5. Use Quality Firewood

Choose well-seasoned hardwoods like oakwood, maplewood, or birchwood. Seasoned wood burns more efficiently and produces less creosote. Store firewood in a dry place, off the ground, and away from the house to prevent insect infestations and mold.

6. Maintain Fireplace Doors and Screens

Wipe down fireplace doors and barriers regularly to remove dust and debris. Check for any signs of damage and repair or replace as needed to keep up safety and aesthetics.
